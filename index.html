FIRST-ORDER MODEL

First-order approximation

\[ \tau \Delta\dot{\theta}(t)+\Delta\theta(t)=w_g(t) \]

Transfer function representation

Taking Laplace transform with zero initial conditions:

\[ \frac{\Delta\Theta(s)}{W_g(s)} = \frac{1}{\tau s+1} \]

Transient response

\( \tau \)
0.632Ag
Larger \( \tau \) means a slower response; smaller \( \tau \) means a faster response.

Step response

\[ W_g(s)=\frac{A_g}{s}, t_g = 0 \]
\[ \Delta\theta(t) = A_g\left(1-e^{-t/\tau}\right) \]

Response to an idealized step gust.

First-order step response adapted from: Ogata, Modern Control Engineering; Nise, Control Systems Engineering.

SECOND-ORDER MODEL

Pitch disturbance model

\[ I_y\Delta\ddot{\theta} +c\Delta\dot{\theta} +k\Delta\theta = w_g(t) \]

Transfer function

\[ \frac{\Delta\Theta(s)}{W_g(s)} = \frac{1}{I_y s^2+cs+k} \]

Normalized transfer function

\[ \frac{\Delta\Theta(s)}{W_g(s)} = \frac{1/I_y} {s^2+2\zeta\omega_n s+\omega_n^2} \]

Key parameters

\[ \omega_n=\sqrt{\frac{k}{I_y}}, \qquad \zeta=\frac{c}{2\sqrt{kI_y}} \]

ωn: Natural frequency of response.
ζ: Damping ratio.

Adapted from Stevens & Lewis, Aircraft Control and Simulation; Ogata, Modern Control Engineering.

UNDERDAMPED SECOND-ORDER RESPONSE: KEY MEASURES

Underdamped step response

Delay time

\[ y(t_d)=0.5\,y_{\infty} \]

Time required to reach 50% of the final value.

Rise time

\[ y(t_r)=y_{\infty} \]

Time to first reach the final value.

Final value

\[ y_{\infty}=\lim_{t\to\infty}y(t) \]

For a unit step response, \(y_{\infty}\) = 1.

Peak time

\[ t_p=\frac{\pi}{\omega_d}, \omega_d = \omega_n\sqrt{1-\zeta^2} \]

Time at which the first peak occurs.

Maximum overshoot

\[ M_p=e^{-\zeta\pi/\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}}\times100\% \]

First peak above \(y_{\infty}\).

Settling time

\[ t_s\approx\frac{4}{\zeta\omega_n} \]

Approximation using the 2% criterion.

Underdamped transient response measures adapted from: Ogata, Modern Control Engineering; Nise, Control Systems Engineering.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

Complex dynamics can be simplified

Response of complex systems such as flight vehicles, to inputs and disturbances, can be effectively captured by first-order or second-order models.

First-order systems are non-oscillatory

The time constant \( \tau \) controls the speed of response.

Second-order systems can oscillate

\( \zeta \) controls damping and overshoot; \( \omega_n \) controls natural response speed.

Performance measures quantify behavior

Performance metrics such as rise time, peak time, settling time, and overshoot enable system comparison.

Transient response analysis tells us not only where the aircraft goes, but how it gets there.
